What It Actually Means
In plain English.
What is MartinLoop?
A safety layer that runs between your team and your AI coding agent.
It sets a cost limit, blocks dangerous moves before they happen, verifies the work actually passed tests, and leaves a signed receipt. Ralph and Ralphy do none of this.
What is Ralph / Ralphy?
AI coding agent runners with no budget control and no safety rails.
Open source loop runners that keep retrying until they succeed or you run out of money. No hard stop. No verifier. No audit trail. Useful starting point, not production-safe.
Why does MartinLoop cost less?
It stops wasting tokens on retries and routes simple tasks to cheaper models first.
Delta re-prompting sends only what changed on retry (not full context). A cheap-model classifier handles trivial tasks before touching expensive models. Real-world runs show 30–60% cost reduction vs unguarded loops (benchmark-cited).
What does "verified completion" mean?
The agent's work actually passed your tests — we didn't just take its word for it.
Ralph and Ralphy report success based on model self-assessment. MartinLoop grades A/B/C based on actual test output. 8/10 patches met the verifier bar; 2/10 were correctly flagged as incomplete.
